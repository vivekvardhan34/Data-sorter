Quick Sort Algorithm

Quick Sort is an efficient, comparison-based sorting algorithm that follows a divide-and-conquer strategy. It works by selecting a "pivot" element from the array and partitioning the other elements into two sub-arrays: those less than the pivot and those greater than it. The pivot is then placed in its correct position in the sorted array. This process is recursively applied to the left and right sub-arrays until the entire array is sorted.
